Imagine an AI Agent capable of creating and orchestrating other AI agents. Herein lies a system where child agents can themselves generate new agents, collaborate across the network, and even regulate their own “reproduction.”

The business potential? Dynamic simulation and strategic insight generation. These agents can explore complex scenarios, uncover patterns and intersections that humans might miss, and generate novel proposals across vast possibility spaces, a form of simulated meta-intelligence.

The Parent Orchestrator defines objectives and manages the agent hierarchy using a policy network controller to assign tasks and track agent states. Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning is also employed to optimize multi-level decision-making across agents. Child Agents perform simulations, generate proposals, and can instantiate sub-agents, sharing knowledge through meta-learning and federated updates. New agents don't just start from a template; they "inherit" the successful behaviors of their parent agents via a genetic algorithm (Steady-state & island model). Agents communicate asynchronously via Kafka in an agent-to-agent framework, while resource use and reproduction are regulated with quotas and container orchestration (Docker Swarm) to ensure scalable, efficient operation across GPUs and cloud nodes. Kafka streams are fed into a Temporally Aware Knowledge Graph (Neo4j) for visualization. As agents spin up sub-agents, they must bid for compute credits based on the predicted "Information Gain" of their task via a global allocator.
